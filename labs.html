How these labs are designed

Every lab is scoped so it can be completed within a focused session, with clear prerequisites, student deliverables, and instructor notes.

Student experience

  • Step-by-step tasks and screenshots in PDF or DOCX format.
  • All labs reference the same AWS account and IAM setup.
  • Students finish with a concrete artifact (script, API, dashboard or report).
  • Difficulty ramps gradually from basic console tasks to automation and AI.

Instructor & LMS use

  • Each lab can map to a single grade item in Brightspace / Moodle.
  • Instructor version includes answer keys, rubrics and troubleshooting notes.
  • Labs can be combined into mini-projects or semester-long capstones.
  • Easy to localise: only AWS console region and screenshots need updating.

Suggested bundles & stages

Group labs into stages to match course weeks, bootcamp days, or corporate training modules.

Stage 1 – Foundation
Labs 1–2 · IAM + first automation
  • Ensure every learner has a safe, working AWS account.
  • Introduce Python + boto3 as the main automation tool.
  • Good starting point for students with no cloud background.
Stage 2 – Governance & tagging
Lab 3 · Policy in practice
  • Turn tagging policies into real, automated enforcement.
  • Connect technical work to cost, ownership and compliance.
Stage 3 – Serverless APIs
Labs 4–5 · Lambda + API Gateway
  • Move from scripts to web-accessible services.
  • Excellent basis for student demos and mini-projects.
Stage 4 – Data & observability
Labs 6–7 · DynamoDB + CloudWatch
  • Store data safely and reason about performance.
  • Teach monitoring and troubleshooting in a realistic way.
Stage 5 – Cost & AI awareness
Labs 8–9 · FinOps + AI workloads
  • Help students understand cost drivers before production.
  • Introduce AI services with concrete, testable examples.
Stage 6 – Capstone
Lab 10 · Integrated mini-project
  • Combine cloud, automation and AI into one narrative.
  • Produce a portfolio artifact for job or promotion discussions.

Delivery & assessment suggestions

A few ideas for how you can map these labs into real teaching and assessment.

In a higher-ed course

  • Use Labs 1–7 as graded weekly activities.
  • Treat Labs 8–9 as formative / practice labs before a project.
  • Map Lab 10 to the final project grade with a clear rubric.
  • Upload lab PDFs and answer keys into your LMS with restricted access.

In corporate / professional training

  • Use Labs 1–5 for a 3-day “AWS Automation with Python” bootcamp.
  • Add Labs 6–9 for a follow-up advanced workshop on observability & AI.
  • Adapt Lab 10 to match the company’s real use case or dataset.
