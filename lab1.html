Lab 1 · Account hygiene & IAM user setup

Track: Cloud & IAM Foundations · Typical duration: 60–90 minutes · Level: Introductory

AWS console Identity & access Secure baseline Pre-requisite for all later labs

1. Objective

Establish a safe, repeatable baseline for working in AWS by creating a dedicated IAM user, enabling multi-factor authentication (MFA) and recording the correct sign-in URL. This removes reliance on the root account for day-to-day work.

2. Business & engineering context

Many incidents and misconfigurations in cloud environments come from shared credentials or over-privileged accounts. This lab sets the tone for disciplined identity and access practices that scale from individual practitioners to full teams.

  • Reduces risk associated with misuse of the root account.
  • Encourages individual ownership and accountability.
  • Aligns hands-on work with governance expectations from day one.

3. Prerequisites

  • Access to an AWS account intended for training or non-production work.
  • Ability to sign in as the account owner or an authorised administrator for initial setup.
  • Authenticator app or MFA method available (phone or hardware token).

4. High-level steps

These are the main stages for the lab. In a full engagement, each step is accompanied by screenshots and precise console paths in the participant workbook.

  • Review the AWS console and locate the IAM service.
  • Create an IAM group intended for engineering or lab work.
  • Attach an appropriate managed policy or custom policy set to the group.
  • Create an individual IAM user and add it to the group.
  • Configure a strong initial password and sign-in experience.
  • Sign in with the new IAM identity and confirm permissions are correct.
  • Enable MFA for the IAM user and validate sign-in with MFA.
  • Record the account-specific sign-in URL and store it securely.

5. Expected outputs

  • A working IAM user with MFA enforced and the correct level of permissions.
  • A captured sign-in URL for the account, ready to be reused in later labs.
  • Short written notes explaining why the root account is no longer needed for daily tasks.

6. Quality criteria

  • The IAM user belongs to the correct group and has no direct, unnecessary policies attached.
  • MFA is enabled and tested with at least one successful sign-in.
  • Permissions are sufficient for later labs, but do not grant full administrative access unless explicitly required.
